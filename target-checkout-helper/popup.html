      Walmart Drop Playbook

      Step 1 — Setup (30 min before drop)

      1. Add each product URL to the monitor list. Paste the OID in the field below the URL — this is your fast lane into the queue.
      2. Set Drop time to 10 seconds before the actual drop. Example: 9:00 PM ET drop → enter 8:59:50 PM. The time picker shows seconds — click the seconds field or use the keyboard to set it. Why: this pre-loads the product tab so the bot is in active polling mode when stock goes live, instead of spending 1–2 seconds loading the page after the drop starts.
      3. Turn on Skip stock monitoring. It won't fire until your drop time — you can set it now and walk away.
      4. Choose your mode:
        • ATC Only ON — bot gets you in the cart, beeps, then stops. You handle checkout. Best if you want control over payment and Place Order.
        • ATC Only OFF — bot handles everything: ATC → cart → checkout → shipping → payment → stops at review. You click Place Order (or turn on Auto place order to go fully hands-off).
      5. Click Start monitoring. Background tabs open — leave them alone.

      Step 2 — While waiting (before drop time)

      • Bot is polling stock every ~250ms as drop time approaches. You'll see a toast on the product tab.
      • Do not close or navigate the product tabs. The bot needs them open and ready.
      • The countdown timers Walmart shows (29:59, 9:59) are placeholders — your real wait is assigned server-side. Ignore them.

      Step 3a — You're IN the queue (bot is waiting)

      The bot detects the queue (ATC button disabled or /qp waiting room page) and holds your position automatically.

      • Do nothing. The bot polls every second for the ATC button to enable. When your turn comes it fires immediately.
      • Do NOT click anywhere on the product page or navigate away — you will lose your queue spot.
      • You'll see a toast updating every 30 seconds with elapsed wait time.
      • When your turn comes: bot fires ATC, navigates to cart, then either stops (ATC Only) or continues to checkout (Full Auto).

      Step 3b — ATC done, bot handed off (ATC Only mode)

      You'll hear a beep and see "✅ ATC done — take over now!" on the cart page.

      1. You're on walmart.com/cart. Verify the item is there.
      2. Click Checkout — this enters Walmart's checkout queue (different from the product queue). Typical wait: 14–31 min.
      3. When the queue clears, Walmart drops you into the checkout flow. Fill shipping and payment, then click Place Order.
      4. If Walmart rejects your card: most common cause is too many authorization attempts from the same card in a short window. Let it cool 5–10 min and retry.

      Step 3c — Full Auto mode (ATC Only OFF)

      Bot fills shipping and payment for you, stops at the review page.

      1. Bot handles ATC → cart → checkout queue wait → shipping → payment automatically.
      2. When the review page loads you'll hear a beep and see "Reached review — Place Order remains manual."
      3. Verify the order (item, price, address, card), then click Place Order.
      4. If Auto place order is ON, the bot clicks Place Order for you — only use this if you trust all your settings are correct.

      Common issues

      • Bot loops on "hang tight / loading" — Walmart's PX bot check. Bot waits for the auto-redirect; do not reload. If it loops for 2+ min, restart monitoring.
      • 456 error / "generating session" loop — your proxy or IP is flagged. Bot auto-rotates if you have proxies set. Otherwise, refresh Walmart manually and let the session re-establish.
      • Invalid card at checkout — too many auth charges from same card in short window. Wait 5–10 min for your bank to unblock it.
      • Queue timer stuck at 29:59 or 9:59 — normal. These are Walmart placeholders. Your real position is being calculated server-side. Don't restart tasks.
      • Item sold out before your turn — the queue cleared but inventory was gone. Nothing to do — try restocks.

      OID (Offer ID) — why it matters

      OID lets the bot add to cart via a direct API call instead of waiting for the page button. Skips 1–2 seconds of rendering — that's earlier queue entry.

      • Get the OID from your monitor service or drop announcement before go-time.
      • Paste it in the OID field under the product URL.
      • Bot tries OID first. If the API rejects it (item not live yet), it falls back to DOM click automatically — no action needed.
      • OID is optional. Without it the bot still works, just enters queue slightly later.

      First-time setup (do once)
      1. Go to chrome://extensions → enable Developer mode → Load unpacked → select the target-checkout-helper folder → pin to toolbar
      2. Sign in to Target (and/or Walmart) in Chrome — make sure your shipping address and credit card are saved on your account
      3. Open this popup → turn the Enable toggle ON
      4. In Checkout options: turn Use saved payment & address → ON
      5. Set Checkout retries → 0 (unlimited) and Retry delay → 1s
      6. Decide: turn Auto place order ON if you want fully automatic checkout, or leave OFF to stop at review and click Place Order yourself
      7. Click Save settings
      Target — Known drop time

      Use this when you know the exact time the item goes live (e.g. a Pokemon card drop at 7:00 AM ET). The bot speeds up polling before the drop and catches stock the instant it appears.

      Step 1: Build cookie pool (30+ min before drop)

      1. In the Cookie harvest section: Harvesting on → ON, Harvests per page load → 3, Data expiration → 8 min, Don't stop harvesting → ON, Apply before checkout → ON
      2. Save settings
      3. Browse to the Target product page — harvesting captures cookies automatically
      4. Click Harvest now for extra snapshots. Build to 30+ in the pool
      5. Click ↻ Check in Checkout options — confirm Logged in: Yes, Address: Saved, Payment: Saved
      6. Make sure your Target cart is empty

      Step 2: Arm the monitor

      1. Turn Don't stop harvesting → OFF (background keepalive handles freshness now)
      2. Save settings
      3. In Product monitor: paste product URL(s), hit +
      4. Set Expected drop / restock to the drop time
      5. Click Start monitoring

      What happens

      1. Poll runs at normal speed, then speeds up to 250ms in the 10 minutes before drop
      2. The instant stock appears, bot navigates to product page → ATC → checkout
      3. Stops at review (or places order if Auto place order is ON)

      While you wait

      • Keep Chrome open (minimized is fine)
      • Do not reload the monitor tab or change settings in the last minute
      • Glance at Snapshots ready — if it hits 0, click Harvest now
      • If Target signs you out, sign back in manually — bot resumes automatically
      Target — Unknown restock / no drop time

      Use this when you don't know when the item restocks. The bot polls at a steady rate until stock appears. Can run for hours or overnight.

      Step 1: Build cookie pool

      1. Same as Known Drop step 1 above, but set Data expiration → 60 min (prevents snapshots from expiring during a long wait)
      2. Build pool to 30+ snapshots

      Step 2: Arm the monitor

      1. Turn Don't stop harvesting → OFF, Save settings
      2. In Product monitor: paste product URL(s), hit +
      3. Leave Expected drop / restock blank — poll runs flat at 500ms
      4. Click Start monitoring

      What happens

      1. Poll runs at a steady 500ms with background keepalive every 5 min
      2. When stock appears (could be minutes, hours, or overnight), bot navigates → ATC → checkout
      3. Consider Endless mode if you want the monitor to restart after each successful checkout
      Walmart — Drop guide

      When to start (9 PM example)

      8:30Log in to Walmart. Navigate to product page. Verify saved address + card.
      8:35Walmart tab → add URL + OID. Set drop time to 8:59:50 (10 sec early). Set max price. Turn on Skip monitoring. Empty cart. Save.
      8:40Click Start monitoring. Tab opens. Hands off.
      8:50Tension window. Do NOT touch settings or reload.
      9:00ATC fires. Bot enters queue.
      9:00+Queue wait (14–31 min). Bot fills shipping → payment → review. You click Place Order.

      Why 20 min early? The tab must be loaded before the tension window at T-10 min. Starting at 8:59 = page still loading when the drop fires.

      Fastest settings

      • Skip monitoring → ON. Fires ATC instantly when button enables.
      • Drop time → set 10 sec early. Pre-loads tab + ramps polling.
      • OID → direct API add-to-cart. Saves 1–2 sec over DOM click.
      • Max price → drop price. Blocks ATC while price is inflated.
      • ATC only → OFF for full auto. ON = you handle checkout.
      • Jig index → 1–99 varies your address. Different index per account.

      Will the tension window flag me?

      No. On Walmart, the bot reads the DOM once per second — zero network requests. The WebSocket queue listener is passive (no outbound traffic). The only request is the single ATC call at drop time. PerimeterX flags fingerprint and mouse patterns, not DOM reads.

      Tabs vs. profiles

      • One tab, one product, one profile. Multiple tabs share one session/cart and will conflict.
      • Multiple items = separate Chrome profiles. Each gets its own cookies, session, settings, and jig index.
      • IMAP 2FA users: each profile has a different extension ID — update allowed_origins in the native host manifest.

      Queue

      • Don't reload. Don't click. Bot holds your position.
      • 29:59 / 9:59 timers are Walmart placeholders — real position is server-side.
      • When your turn comes, WebSocket intercept detects it instantly.
      • 45 min timeout = take over manually. Item gone after queue = sold out.
      Troubleshooting
      • "Target flagged the session — stay on this page." — Target's anti-bot layer flagged you during checkout. Stay put and complete manually. Do NOT reload.
      • "Target may have rejected the session. Clear site data…" — You are NOT in checkout. Clear site data for target.com in Chrome settings, reload, sign in, rebuild cookie pool.
      • "Snapshots ready: 0" — Pool is empty. Open a Target product page or click Harvest now to refill.
      • "Sign in if you see a prompt" — Sign in manually in the tab. Bot resumes automatically.
      • ATC button stays disabled — Item is out of stock. Bot is in stock-watch mode, polling until it appears.
      • Chrome banner "has started debugging this browser" — Normal. Appears briefly when ATC click fires. Goes away after.
      • "Price too high — waiting for drop price" (Walmart) — Max price guard is active. Price will update at drop time.
      • "In queue — waiting…" (Walmart) — Normal. Bot is holding your queue position. Wait it out.
      • "Queue wait exceeded 45 min" (Walmart) — Unusual. Consider taking over manually.
